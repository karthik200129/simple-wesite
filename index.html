    TWIN CITY HISTORY

     Hubli (or Hubballi) and Dharwad are twin cities in the Indian state Karnataka. Hubballi-Dharwad  form the second-largest municipality and urban agglomeration of Karnataka in terms of population, after the capital Bangalore.

    While Dharwad is the administrative headquarters, the city of Hubli, situated about 20 km (12 mi) south-east of Dharwad, is the commercial centre and business hub of North Karnataka.

    Hubballi-Dharwad remains one of the largest urban agglomerations in Karnataka and an important center for education, culture, and trade in southern India. The cities continue to grow, with improvements in infrastructure, such as the Hubballi Airport and the Hubballi-Dharwad Bus Rapid Transit System (BRTS).

    DID YOU KNOW ?

    Hubballi-Dharwad BRTS System

    The Hubballi-Dharwad Bus Rapid Transit System (BRTS) is a significant public transportation project aimed at improving urban mobility in the twin cities of Hubballi and Dharwad in Karnataka.
    The BRTS corridor spans about 22.25 kilometers, connecting Hubballi and Dharwad. It runs from the Hubballi CBT (Central Bus Terminus) to Dharwad CBT, passing through key areas along the route.
    It was designed to offer a more efficient, reliable, and sustainable alternative to the growing traffic congestion caused by the region's rapid urbanization.

    Chota Mumbai Hubballi

    Hubballi is called chota Mumbai in Karnataka. It is a commercial town in north Karnataka, the central hub for South Western Railways and is a house to many traders from various parts of Gujarat, Maharashtra & Rajasthan hence it is known as Chota Bombay.
    Hubballi has a vibrant cultural mix, attracting people from different parts of the state and country, just like Mumbai. Its population is diverse, with various languages spoken, including Kannada, Marathi, Hindi, and English, which gives it a cosmopolitan feel similar to Mumbai.

    Indian Flag Manufacturing

    The Karnataka Khadi Gramodyoga Samyukta Sangha (KKGSS) is a manufacturing federation located in Garag village near Dharwad City and also its headquarters in Bengeri of Hubli City. It is one of the five units in India that are authorised by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) to manufacture and supply the Indian flag.
    KKGSS has the capacity to produce thousands of flags annually, especially during times of national importance like Independence Day (August 15) and Republic Day (January 26). The unit operates at peak capacity during these times to meet the high demand for flags across the country.

    World’s Longest Railway Platform in HUBBALLI

    Indian Railways' South Western Railway(SWR) zone's Hubballi has its name registered in the Guinness Book of World Records now, for having the world's longest railway platform at Shri Siddharoodha Swamiji station.It is a 1507 m long platform that has been built at a cost of about INR 20 crore.
    Hubballi is a key railway hub in Karnataka and plays a vital role in connecting major cities like Bengaluru, Mumbai, Delhi, and Hyderabad. With the expansion, the station is now better equipped to handle more trains and cater to the increasing passenger traffic.
