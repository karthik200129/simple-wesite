History OF Hubballi-Dharwad

Pre-Historic Roots

Early Kingdoms: Over the centuries, the region was ruled by various South Indian dynasties. The Chalukyas of Badami (6th–8th centuries CE) were among the earliest rulers, and later the Rashtrakutas and Hoysalas also controlled the area.

Vijayanagara Empire: In the 14th century, the region came under the dominance of the Vijayanagara Empire, which was one of South India’s most powerful empires. Hubballi and Dharwad were strategically important towns during this perio.

British Colonial Era

Dharwad as a Hub: The British East India Company annexed the region after defeating the Marathas in the early 19th century. Dharwad became the headquarters of the Dharwad district under the Bombay Presidency during British rule. It was an important center for education, administration, and trade.

Railway Connectivity: During the British period, Hubballi (or Hubli) grew into a significant commercial town, especially with the development of the railway network. It became an important trading center for cotton, iron, and other goods..

Post-Independence

Merging of the Cities: Hubballi and Dharwad were formally combined into a single municipal corporation in 1962. While Hubballi developed as a major commercial and industrial hub, Dharwad retained its charm as an educational and cultural center.

Economic Growth: Hubballi, known for its trading and industrial significance, houses one of the largest railway workshops in India, while Dharwad became the seat of several academic institutions like Karnatak University, Karnataka law University & University of Agricultural Sciences .
